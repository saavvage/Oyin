# First Aid for Sports Injuries

## RICE Protocol

For acute soft tissue injuries (sprains, strains, bruises), apply the RICE protocol within the first 48-72 hours:

### Rest
- Stop the activity immediately
- Avoid putting weight on the injured area
- Use crutches for lower limb injuries if needed

### Ice
- Apply ice wrapped in a thin towel (never directly on skin)
- Apply for 15-20 minutes
- Repeat every 2-3 hours while awake
- Continue for 48-72 hours

### Compression
- Use an elastic bandage
- Wrap firmly but not too tight
- Check for numbness, tingling, or color change
- Loosen if these occur

### Elevation
- Raise the injured area above heart level
- Use pillows or cushions for support
- Maintain elevation as much as possible

## Bleeding Control

### Minor Cuts and Scrapes
1. Clean hands before treating
2. Rinse wound with clean water
3. Apply gentle pressure with clean cloth
4. Apply antibiotic ointment
5. Cover with sterile bandage

### Severe Bleeding
1. Call emergency services
2. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
3. Do not remove cloth if blood soaks through - add more layers
4. Keep victim lying down
5. Maintain pressure until help arrives

## Head Injuries

### Concussion Recognition
Signs and symptoms include:
- Headache or pressure in head
- Confusion or feeling foggy
- Dizziness or balance problems
- Nausea or vomiting
- Sensitivity to light or noise
- Memory problems
- Feeling sluggish or groggy

### Concussion Response
1. Remove person from play immediately
2. Do not leave them alone
3. Seek medical evaluation
4. Do not allow return to sport until cleared by healthcare provider

**Red Flags Requiring Emergency Care:**
- Loss of consciousness
- Seizures
- Worsening headache
- Repeated vomiting
- Slurred speech
- Weakness or numbness
- Unequal pupils

## Heat-Related Emergencies

### Heat Exhaustion
**Symptoms:** Heavy sweating, weakness, cold/pale/clammy skin, fast weak pulse, nausea, fainting

**Treatment:**
1. Move to cool location
2. Lie down and elevate legs
3. Apply cool, wet cloths
4. Sip water slowly
5. Seek medical attention if vomiting or symptoms worsen

### Heat Stroke
**Symptoms:** High body temperature (>40°C), hot/red/dry skin, rapid strong pulse, confusion, unconsciousness

**Treatment:**
1. Call emergency services immediately
2. Move to cool area
3. Cool rapidly with any means available (cold water, ice packs to neck/armpits/groin)
4. Do not give fluids if unconscious

## Muscle Cramps

### Immediate Treatment
1. Stop activity
2. Gently stretch the affected muscle
3. Massage the muscle
4. Apply ice if cramping persists
5. Hydrate with water or electrolyte drink

### Prevention
- Adequate hydration before, during, and after activity
- Proper warm-up
- Gradual conditioning
- Adequate sodium intake for prolonged exercise

## Fracture Suspicion

### Signs
- Deformity or abnormal position
- Severe pain and swelling
- Inability to move or bear weight
- Grinding sensation
- Bone visible through skin (open fracture)

### Response
1. Do not move the person unless necessary
2. Immobilize the area above and below suspected fracture
3. Apply ice wrapped in cloth
4. Treat for shock if necessary
5. Seek emergency medical care

## Eye Injuries

### Foreign Object
1. Do not rub the eye
2. Flush with clean water
3. If object remains, cover eye and seek medical attention

### Direct Impact
1. Apply cold compress gently
2. Do not apply pressure
3. Seek medical evaluation for vision changes or severe pain

## Emergency Action Plan

Every sports venue should have:
- First aid kit accessible
- Emergency contact numbers posted
- AED location known
- Clear access for emergency vehicles
- At least one person trained in first aid present
