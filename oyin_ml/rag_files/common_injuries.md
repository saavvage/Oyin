# Common Sports Injuries and Prevention

## Muscle Strains

### Description
A muscle strain occurs when muscle fibers are overstretched or torn. Common locations include hamstrings, quadriceps, and calf muscles.

### Symptoms
- Sudden pain during activity
- Swelling or bruising
- Limited range of motion
- Muscle weakness

### Prevention
- Adequate warm-up before activity
- Regular flexibility training
- Progressive training load increases (no more than 10% per week)
- Proper hydration and nutrition

### Initial Treatment (RICE Protocol)
- **Rest**: Avoid activities that cause pain
- **Ice**: Apply for 15-20 minutes every 2-3 hours for first 48 hours
- **Compression**: Use elastic bandage to reduce swelling
- **Elevation**: Keep injured area above heart level when possible

## Joint Sprains

### Description
A sprain is an injury to ligaments, the tissues connecting bones at a joint. Ankle sprains are most common in sports.

### Symptoms
- Pain and tenderness around the joint
- Swelling and bruising
- Instability or difficulty bearing weight
- Popping sensation at time of injury

### Prevention
- Wear appropriate footwear for your sport
- Use ankle braces if history of sprains
- Strengthen surrounding muscles
- Practice balance and proprioception exercises

## Knee Injuries

### Common Types
- **Patellofemoral syndrome**: Pain around or behind the kneecap
- **ACL/MCL injuries**: Ligament tears from sudden direction changes
- **Meniscus tears**: Cartilage damage from twisting movements

### Prevention
- Strengthen quadriceps and hamstrings equally
- Practice proper landing technique (knees over toes)
- Avoid sudden increases in training intensity
- Use proper footwear with adequate support

### Warning Signs Requiring Medical Attention
- Knee gives way or feels unstable
- Significant swelling within hours of injury
- Unable to bear weight
- Locking or catching sensation

## Tennis/Golfer's Elbow

### Description
Overuse injuries affecting tendons in the forearm. Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) affects the outside of the elbow; golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis) affects the inside.

### Prevention
- Use proper grip size on racquets/clubs
- Strengthen forearm muscles
- Use proper technique (lessons from qualified instructor)
- Take breaks during repetitive activities
- Gradual return after time off

## Shoulder Injuries

### Common in
Racquet sports, swimming, throwing sports

### Prevention
- Strengthen rotator cuff muscles
- Maintain good posture
- Avoid overhead activities when fatigued
- Include shoulder mobility work in warm-up

## When to Seek Medical Attention

Seek immediate medical care for:
- Obvious deformity
- Inability to bear weight or use the limb
- Severe swelling
- Numbness or tingling
- Signs of infection (fever, red streaks, warmth)

Seek non-emergency medical evaluation for:
- Pain persisting more than 2 weeks
- Recurring injuries in same location
- Joint instability
- Pain that worsens despite rest
