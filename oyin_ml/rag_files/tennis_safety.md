# Tennis-Specific Health and Safety

## Common Tennis Injuries

### Tennis Elbow (Lateral Epicondylitis)
The most prevalent tennis injury, affecting 40-50% of recreational players at some point.

**Risk Factors:**
- Improper backhand technique (leading with elbow)
- Grip too small or too large
- Racquet too heavy or too stiff
- String tension too high
- Playing too frequently without rest

**Prevention:**
- Use a two-handed backhand or proper one-handed technique
- Get racquet professionally fitted
- Consider lower string tension (48-52 lbs for recreational players)
- Strengthen wrist and forearm muscles
- Ice elbow after play if any discomfort

### Shoulder Injuries

**Common Issues:**
- Rotator cuff tendinitis
- Shoulder impingement
- Labral tears (severe cases)

**Prevention:**
- Include shoulder strengthening in training routine
- Avoid excessive overhead serving when fatigued
- Use proper serving technique with full body rotation
- Limit serve practice to 50-100 serves per session

### Ankle Sprains

Tennis involves frequent lateral movements and sudden direction changes.

**Prevention:**
- Wear tennis-specific shoes with lateral support
- Replace shoes every 45-60 hours of play
- Practice lateral movement drills
- Consider ankle braces if history of sprains

### Knee Injuries

**Prevention:**
- Strengthen quadriceps and hamstrings
- Practice proper split-step technique
- Avoid playing on extremely hard courts if possible
- Use knee brace if experiencing patellofemoral pain

## Playing Surface Considerations

### Hard Courts
- Highest impact on joints
- Faster ball speed
- Increase warm-up time
- Consider cushioned insoles

### Clay Courts
- Lower impact, easier on joints
- Sliding technique required (injury risk if improper)
- Longer rallies may increase fatigue

### Grass Courts
- Variable bounce requires alertness
- Slippery when wet - avoid play
- Lower impact than hard courts

## Heat and Sun Safety for Tennis

Tennis matches can last several hours in direct sun.

**Recommendations:**
- Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen, reapply every 2 hours
- Wear a hat or visor
- Take full rest time during changeovers
- Drink water at every changeover (150-250ml)
- In extreme heat (>35°C), consider rescheduling

**Heat Exhaustion Signs:**
- Heavy sweating
- Cold, pale, clammy skin
- Fast, weak pulse
- Nausea or vomiting
- Muscle cramps

If experienced, stop play, move to shade, and hydrate. If symptoms persist beyond 30 minutes, seek medical attention.

## Age-Specific Considerations

### Junior Players (Under 18)
- Limit serve practice to prevent shoulder/elbow overuse
- Ensure proper growth-appropriate equipment
- Emphasize technique over power
- Mandatory rest days between tournament matches

### Senior Players (Over 50)
- Extended warm-up (15-20 minutes)
- Consider doubles to reduce court coverage
- Allow longer recovery between sessions
- Pay attention to early warning signs of strain

## Equipment Safety

- Inspect racquet for cracks before play
- Replace worn grips to prevent slipping
- Check court for hazards (wet spots, debris) before play
- Ensure proper court lighting for evening play
