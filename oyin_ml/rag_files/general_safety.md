# General Sports Safety Guidelines

## Pre-Activity Assessment

Before engaging in any physical sport activity, individuals should assess their current health status. Those with pre-existing conditions such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or respiratory conditions should consult a healthcare provider before starting new sports activities.

## Hydration Requirements

Proper hydration is essential for safe sports participation. Adults should consume approximately 500ml of water 2 hours before exercise. During activity, drink 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes. After exercise, replace fluid losses by drinking 1.5 liters for every kilogram of body weight lost.

Signs of dehydration include:
- Dark yellow urine
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Dry mouth and excessive thirst
- Fatigue and reduced performance
- Headache

## Temperature Considerations

Exercise in extreme temperatures requires additional precautions. In hot conditions (above 30°C), reduce intensity and increase rest periods. In cold conditions (below 5°C), ensure proper warm-up duration is extended by 5-10 minutes.

Heat-related illness warning signs:
- Excessive sweating followed by no sweating
- Nausea or vomiting
- Confusion or disorientation
- Rapid heartbeat
- Body temperature above 40°C

If heat stroke is suspected, stop activity immediately, move to a cool area, and seek emergency medical attention.

## Rest and Recovery

Adequate rest between training sessions prevents overuse injuries. General guidelines recommend at least 48 hours between intense sessions targeting the same muscle groups. Sleep 7-9 hours per night for optimal recovery.

## When to Stop Activity

Stop physical activity immediately if experiencing:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe shortness of breath
- Dizziness or fainting
- Severe joint or muscle pain
- Irregular heartbeat
