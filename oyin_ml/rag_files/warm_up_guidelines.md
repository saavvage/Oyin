# Warm-Up and Cool-Down Guidelines

## Purpose of Warm-Up

A proper warm-up prepares the body for physical activity by gradually increasing heart rate, blood flow to muscles, and joint mobility. This reduces injury risk and improves performance.

## General Warm-Up Structure

A complete warm-up should last 10-15 minutes and include three phases:

### Phase 1: Light Aerobic Activity (5 minutes)
- Jogging in place
- Jumping jacks
- Brisk walking
- Light cycling

Goal: Raise heart rate to 50-60% of maximum.

### Phase 2: Dynamic Stretching (5 minutes)
- Leg swings (forward/backward and side-to-side)
- Arm circles
- Hip rotations
- Lunges with rotation
- High knees
- Butt kicks

Avoid static stretching before activity as it may reduce power output.

### Phase 3: Sport-Specific Movement (3-5 minutes)
Perform movements that mimic the upcoming activity at gradually increasing intensity.

## Sport-Specific Warm-Up Additions

### Racquet Sports (Tennis, Badminton, Squash)
- Wrist circles and forearm stretches
- Shadow swings at 50% then 75% intensity
- Short court rallies before full court play
- Lateral shuffles and split steps

### Running/Jogging
- A-skips and B-skips
- Gradual pace increase over first 5-10 minutes
- Strides at 80% effort

### Team Sports (Basketball, Football, Volleyball)
- Sport-specific drills at low intensity
- Passing and catching exercises
- Directional changes and pivots

## Cool-Down Guidelines

Cool-down should last 5-10 minutes and include:

1. **Gradual intensity reduction**: Slow jogging or walking for 3-5 minutes
2. **Static stretching**: Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds
   - Quadriceps stretch
   - Hamstring stretch
   - Calf stretch
   - Hip flexor stretch
   - Shoulder and arm stretches
3. **Deep breathing**: 1-2 minutes of controlled breathing

## Warning Signs During Warm-Up

If you experience any of these during warm-up, do not proceed with full activity:
- Sharp pain in joints or muscles
- Unusual shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Chest discomfort

These may indicate underlying issues requiring medical evaluation.
